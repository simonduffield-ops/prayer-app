The Examen

"And whatever you do, whether in word or deed, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him." - Colossians 3:17 (NIV)

1
Give Thanks - Gratitude is the Foundation

Recall and thank God for the blessings of the day. Think through your day like watching a movie. What moments brought you joy? What gifts did God provide? Thank Him for both the obvious blessings and the small graces you might have missed.

Write or reflect on 3-5 things you're grateful for from today...

2
Ask for Light - Invite the Holy Spirit

Pray for the Holy Spirit to help you see your day clearly, with honesty and openness. Ask God to illuminate the movements of consolation and desolation in your heart today.

"Holy Spirit, give me light to see this day through Your eyes and discern Your movements in my soul..."

3
Review the Day - Notice Consolation and Desolation

Walk through the events of the day, paying attention to your inner movements:

Consolation: When did you feel close to God? Notice moments of peace, joy, hope, faith, and charity. What experiences drew you toward love of God and neighbor? What inflamed your soul with spiritual warmth?

Ask: "What brought me closer to God today?"

Desolation: When did you feel distant from God or resistant to His presence? Notice spiritual dryness, darkness, agitation, or movement toward earthly things. What pulled you into self-absorption or away from love?

Ask: "What distracted me or pulled me away from God today?"

4
Face Your Shortcomings - Ask Forgiveness

Recognize times you failed to love, made poor choices, or missed God's presence. Where did you fall short? Bring these honestly to Jesus with humility and receive His grace and forgiveness.

Confess your shortcomings and receive God's mercy...

5
Look Ahead - Prepare for Tomorrow

Turn your thoughts toward tomorrow: what challenges or opportunities await? Based on your recognition of consolation and desolation today, ask for God's grace. What did you learn about what draws you closer to God? How can you approach tomorrow with His help?

Ask God for grace to pursue what brings life and wisdom to navigate what challenges you...

"Father, thank You for walking with me today. Help me to rest in Your love tonight and to trust You with tomorrow. Amen."

Affirmation Prayer

1
Adoration
"Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come." - Revelation 4:8 (NIV)
2
God's Promise for Today
"For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life." - John 3:16
3
Prayer

Abba, Father. This is the day that you have created. I choose to rejoice and be glad in it. There may be many challenges, but I choose to believe the truth that you are with me and only have my best in mind. I choose to believe that you are always working in my life for my benefit.

 

Thank you for opening the way for me to be in oneness with you through the death of Jesus on the cross, his burial in the tomb, his resurrection on the third day, his asension, and his pouring out of the Holy Spirit. I commit my life afresh to you this day and submit to the Lordship of Jesus.

 

Thank you that Jesus is in you and that you are in Jesus and that your Spirit dwells in me. Thank you that when You look upon me, you only see righteousness, worthiness, holiness, and purity because Jesus cancelled all my guilt and shame on the cross.

 

Thank you that you created me to be exactly who you want me to be and that I am your child and beautiful in your sight. Today, I choose to see myself as you see me, to believe in myself as you believe in me and to love myself as you love me.

 

Your mercies are new every morning, and I receive your abundance and generous grace for this day.

 

Thank you that the resurrection power of your presence is living in me.

Thank you for your counsel and comfort every minute of every day.

Thank you for guiding me continually in the flow of God's forgiveness and mercy.

 

I open my heart to receive all the ways you want to communicate with me, and to receive everything you desire for me today.

Help me to walk in your light, that I may please you in every way, and bear fruit in every good work.

Help me to give myself away to others, being kind to everyone I meet, loving the lost, and proclaiming Christ in all I do and say.

 

Father, I bind my will to your will, obligating it to your plans and purposes for me.

I bind my mind to the mind of Jesus so I can hear your thoughts in my thoughts.

I bind my emotions to the healing balm and comfort of the Holy Spirit.

 

I loose, smash, crush and destroy all the wrong beliefs, wrong attitudes, excuses, deception and denial that my soul has been holding onto. I loose the effects and influences any wrong words spoken about me or to me and I loose the works of the enemy from me.

 

Help me to be aware of you and your love throughout this day.

 

Today Abba, Father, your will be done on earth as it is in Heaven.

Amen

4
Listen to the First Question

"Abba, Father, what is the scheme of the enemy in my life today?"

Be still and listen. What comes to mind? Don't rush. God may speak through a word, phrase, image, memory, or gentle impression.

Write or reflect on what you sense God saying...

5
Listen to the Second Question

"Abba, Father, what do you want me to know about today?"

Sometimes God wants to reveal something about Himself, about you, or about a situation. Listen for His wisdom and insight.

What is God revealing to you?

6
Listen to the Third Question

"Abba, Father, what do you want me to do?"

God's voice always leads to action. What specific step is He inviting you to take? This may be big or small, immediate or in the future.

What action is God calling you to?

7
Give Thanks

Take time to thank God for speaking to you, for His guidance, and for His presence with you today.

Express your gratitude to God for what He has revealed...

Choose a Prayer

Prayer of St. Francis

1912

Despite popular attribution to St. Francis of Assisi, this prayer was first published anonymously in 1912 in a French spiritual magazine. It became widely beloved during both World Wars and was used by Mother Teresa, Desmond Tutu, Margaret Thatcher, and many others as an anthem of peace and service.

Lord, make me an instrument of your peace:
Where there is hatred, let me sow love;
Where there is injury, pardon;
Where there is doubt, faith;
Where there is despair, hope;
Where there is darkness, light;
And where there is sadness, joy.

O Divine Master, grant that I may
Not so much seek to be consoled as to console,
To be understood as to understand,
To be loved as to love.

For it is in giving that we receive,
It is in pardoning that we are pardoned,
And it is in dying that we are born to eternal life.
Amen.

The Serenity Prayer

Reinhold Niebuhr, 1932

Written by Protestant theologian Reinhold Niebuhr around 1932, this prayer became famous through Alcoholics Anonymous starting in 1941 and has since become one of the most widely known prayers worldwide.

Short Version:

God, grant me the serenity
To accept the things I cannot change,
The courage to change the things I can,
And the wisdom to know the difference.
Amen.

Full Version:

God, give us grace to accept with serenity
the things that cannot be changed,
Courage to change the things
which should be changed,
and the Wisdom to distinguish
the one from the other.

Living one day at a time,
Enjoying one moment at a time,
Accepting hardship as a pathway to peace,
Taking, as Jesus did,
This sinful world as it is,
Not as I would have it,
Trusting that You will make all things right,
If I surrender to Your will,
So that I may be reasonably happy in this life,
And supremely happy with You forever in the next.
Amen.

St. Ignatius, founder of the Jesuits, used this prayer to encourage total commitment to God and service.

The Suscipe (Take, Lord, Receive)

St. Ignatius of Loyola, 16th Century

Another profound prayer from St. Ignatius, expressing complete surrender to God. This prayer appears in his Spiritual Exercises and is often prayed during retreats and at times of major life decisions.

My memory, my understanding,
And my entire will,

You have given all to me.
To You, Lord, I return it.

Everything is Yours; do with it what You will.
Give me only Your love and Your grace.
That is enough for me.
Amen.

The Doxology

Thomas Ken, c. 1674

Possibly the single most widely-sung verse in Christian history. Written by Bishop Thomas Ken as a concluding verse for his morning, evening, and midnight hymns. Its theological completeness, simplicity, and the strong tune OLD HUNDREDTH made it Christianity's most universal prayer across virtually all traditions.

Praise God, from whom all blessings flow;
Praise Him, all creatures here below;
Praise Him above, ye heavenly host;
Praise Father, Son, and Holy Ghost. Amen.

Radiating Christ

Mother Teresa (adapted from Cardinal Newman)

A beautiful intergenerational connection: Cardinal Newman originally wrote this as a meditation, which Mother Teresa adapted and changed to plural pronouns for her sisters to pray communally. Recited daily after Mass by the Missionaries of Charity since 1950, it embodies transparent witness and the catching force of love.

Dear Jesus, help us to spread Your fragrance everywhere we go.
Flood our souls with Your Spirit and Life.
Penetrate and possess our whole being so utterly
that our lives may only be a radiance of Yours.

Shine through us and be so in us
that every soul we come in contact with
may feel Your presence in our soul.
Let them look up and see no longer us, but only Jesus.

Stay with us and then we shall begin to shine as You shine,
so to shine as to be a light to others.

Daily Listening

"Whether you turn to the right or to the left, your ears will hear a voice behind you, saying, 'This is the way; walk in it.'" - Isaiah 30:21 (NIV)

1
Prepare Your Heart

Take a few deep breaths. Quiet your mind. Ask the Holy Spirit to speak to you. Remember: God loves you and desires to communicate with you.

"Holy Spirit, help me to hear Your voice clearly..."

2
Listen to the First Question

"What is the most important thing you want to tell me right now/today?"

Be still and listen. What comes to mind? Don't rush. God may speak through a word, phrase, image, memory, or gentle impression.

Write or reflect on what you sense God saying...

3
Listen to the Second Question

"What do you want me to know?"

Sometimes God wants to reveal something about Himself, about you, or about a situation. Listen for His wisdom and insight.

What is God revealing to you?

4
Listen to the Third Question

"What do you want me to do?"

God's voice always leads to action. What specific step is He inviting you to take? This may be big or small, immediate or in the future.

What action is God calling you to?

"Nothing carries more potential for change than individual acts of human kindness." - Jamie Winship
"Lord, thank You for speaking to me. Give me the courage and faith to walk in what You've shown me. Amen."
