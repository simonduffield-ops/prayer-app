The Examen

"And whatever you do, whether in word or deed, do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him." - Colossians 3:17 (NIV)

The Ignatian Examen is a practice of prayerful reflection that helps you recognize consolation (movements drawing you toward God) and desolation (movements pulling you away from Him). By discerning these movements, you learn to recognize God's voice and resist what draws you away from Him.

1
Give Thanks - Gratitude is the Foundation

Recall and thank God for the blessings of the day. Think through your day like watching a movie. What moments brought you joy? What gifts did God provide? Thank Him for both the obvious blessings and the small graces you might have missed.

Write or reflect on 3-5 things you're grateful for from today...

2
Ask for Light - Invite the Holy Spirit

Pray for the Holy Spirit to help you see your day clearly, with honesty and openness. Ask God to illuminate the movements of consolation and desolation in your heart today.

"Holy Spirit, give me light to see this day through Your eyes and discern Your movements in my soul..."

3
Review the Day - Notice Consolation and Desolation

Walk through the events of the day, paying attention to your inner movements:

Consolation: When did you feel close to God? Notice moments of peace, joy, hope, faith, and charity. What experiences drew you toward love of God and neighbor? What inflamed your soul with spiritual warmth?

Ask: "What brought me closer to God today?"

Desolation: When did you feel distant from God or resistant to His presence? Notice spiritual dryness, darkness, agitation, or movement toward earthly things. What pulled you into self-absorption or away from love?

Ask: "What distracted me or pulled me away from God today?"

4
Face Your Shortcomings - Ask Forgiveness

Recognize times you failed to love, made poor choices, or missed God's presence. Where did you fall short? Bring these honestly to Jesus with humility and receive His grace and forgiveness.

Confess your shortcomings and receive God's mercy...

5
Look Ahead - Prepare for Tomorrow

Turn your thoughts toward tomorrow: what challenges or opportunities await? Based on your recognition of consolation and desolation today, ask for God's grace. What did you learn about what draws you closer to God? How can you approach tomorrow with His help?

Ask God for grace to pursue what brings life and wisdom to navigate what challenges you...

"Father, thank You for walking with me today. Help me to rest in Your love tonight and to trust You with tomorrow. Amen."

Lectio Divina

"Blessed is the one who does not walk in step with the wicked... whose delight is in the law of the Lord, and who meditates on his law day and night." - Psalm 1:1-2 (NIV)

"Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and lowly in heart, and you will find rest for your souls." - Matthew 11:28-29
1
Lectio - Read

Read the passage above slowly and attentively. What word or phrase stands out to you? Read it again, even more slowly.

Write the word or phrase that catches your attention...

2
Meditatio - Meditate

Repeat your word or phrase quietly. What is God saying to you through these words? How do they connect to your life right now?

Reflect on what God might be speaking to you...

3
Oratio - Pray

Respond to God in prayer. Share your heart - your joys, concerns, needs, and gratitude. Let this passage shape your prayer.

Pray in response to God's word...

4
Contemplatio - Rest

Simply rest in God's presence. Let go of words and thoughts. Allow His love to surround you in silence.

Rest silently in God's presence for 2-3 minutes...

Focus Word: "Rest"
Carry this word with you today as a reminder of God's invitation.

Acknowledge - See God's Holiness

What do you see about God's character in this passage?

Transformation - Be Changed by His Glory

"I keep asking that the God of our Lord Jesus Christ, the glorious Father, may give you the Spirit of wisdom and revelation, so that you may know him better. The Lord's Prayer

"This, then, is how you should pray: 'Our Father in heaven...'" - Matthew 6:9 (NIV)

Journey through the Lord's Prayer as a framework for worship, intercession, and spiritual warfare. Each movement invites you deeper into communion with God.

1
Our Father Who Is In Heaven
Approach, Worship, Adoration & Surrender
"Therefore, brothers and sisters, since we have confidence to enter the Most Holy Place by the blood of Jesus... let us draw near to God with a sincere heart and with the full assurance that faith brings." - Hebrews 10:19,22

Reflect: Come into God's presence as a beloved child. He is your Father - both intimately close and infinitely glorious. Worship Him for who He is. Surrender your heart, your day, and your will to Him.

Take time to worship and adore your Heavenly Father...

2
Hallowed Be Your Name
Worship, Gratitude & Prayer for the World
"Ascribe to the Lord the glory due his name; worship the Lord in the splendor of his holiness." - Psalm 29:2

Reflect: Honor God's holy name. Thank Him for who He has revealed Himself to be. Pray that the world would know Him as He truly is - in all His beauty, holiness, and love.

Worship God's character and pray for His name to be honored throughout the earth...

3
Your Kingdom Come, Your Will Be Done
Submission & Intercession
"Seek first his kingdom and his righteousness, and all these things will be given to you as well." - Matthew 6:33

Reflect: Submit to God's reign in your life. Pray for His kingdom to come in your family, your church, the persecuted church worldwide, and for revival and renewal in the nations.

Intercede for God's will to be done on earth as it is in heaven...

4
Give Us This Day Our Daily Bread
Provision - His Presence, Mercy & Help
"And my God will meet all your needs according to the riches of his glory in Christ Jesus." - Philippians 4:19

Reflect: Ask for God's provision - His presence above all, His mercy for today, physical provision, and help in every area of need. Acknowledge your complete dependence on Him.

Bring your needs to God, trusting in His faithful provision...

5
Forgive Us As We Forgive Others
Reflection & Release
"Bear with each other and forgive one another if any of you has a grievance against someone. Forgive as the Lord forgave you." - Colossians 3:13

Reflect: Receive God's forgiveness for your sins. Then release everyone and everything to Him - forgiving those who have hurt you, letting go of offenses, surrendering control.

Confess your sins, receive forgiveness, and extend forgiveness to others...

6
Lead Us Not Into Trial, Deliver Us From Evil
Gethsemane Prayer & Spiritual Warfare
"Submit yourselves, then, to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you." - James 4:7

Reflect: Like Jesus in Gethsemane, submit to God's will even in difficulty. Stand against spiritual forces of evil. Ask for protection from temptation and deliverance from the evil one.

Pray for strength in trials and victory over spiritual opposition...

7
Listening Prayer
Hearing God's Voice
"Whether you turn to the right or to the left, your ears will hear a voice behind you, saying, 'This is the way; walk in it.'" - Isaiah 30:21

Ask God:

"What is important for me to know about today?"

"What do You want me to do about it?"

Be still and listen for God's gentle voice...

"For Yours is the kingdom and the power and the glory forever. Amen."

who was conceived by the Holy Spirit
and born of the virgin Mary.

He ascended to heaven
and is seated at the right hand of God the Father almighty.
From there he will come to judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit,
the holy catholic church,
the communion of saints,
the forgiveness of sins,
the resurrection of the body,
and the life everlasting.

Amen.

The Nicene Creed

Formulated at the Council of Nicaea (325 AD) and Constantinople (381 AD)

We believe in one God,
the Father, the almighty,
maker of heaven and earth,
of all that is, seen and unseen.

We believe in one Lord, Jesus Christ,
the only Son of God,
eternally begotten of the Father,
God from God, Light from Light,
true God from true God,
begotten, not made,
of one being with the Father.
Through him all things were made.
For us men and for our salvation
he came down from heaven;
by the power of the Holy Spirit
he became incarnate of the Virgin Mary,
and was made man.
For our sake he was crucified under Pontius Pilate;
he suffered death and was buried.
On the third day he rose again
in accordance with the scriptures;
he ascended into heaven
and is seated at the right hand of the father.
He will come again in glory to judge the living and the dead,
and his kingdom will have no end.

We believe in the Holy Spirit, the Lord, the giver of life,
who proceeds from the Father and the Son.
With the Father and the Son he is worshipped and glorified.
He has spoken through the Prophets.

We believe in one holy catholic and apostolic Church.
We acknowledge one baptism for the forgiveness of sins.
We look for the resurrection of the dead,
and the life of the world to come.

Amen.

Affirm Your Faith

Read the creed aloud, slowly and thoughtfully. Notice the corporate "we" - you are declaring this faith alongside the universal church.

Speak or read the creed as your own confession of faith...

2
Meditate on the Truth

Reflect on one part of the creed that speaks to your heart today...

Give Thanks

Express gratitude for the truths you've declared...

"Triune God - Father, Son, and Holy Spirit - I confess You as the one true God. May these truths shape my mind, guide my life, and fill me with worship. Amen."

Written Prayers

"The prayer of a righteous person is powerful and effective." - James 5:16 (NIV)

A collection of 10 profound and enduring prayers that have shaped Christian spirituality across centuries and denominations. Join countless believers in praying these timeless words.

Choose a Prayer

The Lord's Prayer

1st Century AD

The most foundational Christian prayer, taught by Jesus himself to his disciples. Found in Matthew 6:9-13 and Luke 11:2-4.

Our Father, who art in heaven,
Hallowed be thy Name.
Thy Kingdom come.
Thy will be done on earth, as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread.
And forgive us our trespasses,
As we forgive those who trespass against us.
And lead us not into temptation,
But deliver us from evil.
For thine is the kingdom, the power, and the glory,
For ever and ever. Amen.

Prayer of St. Francis

1912

Despite popular attribution to St. Francis of Assisi, this prayer was first published anonymously in 1912 in a French spiritual magazine. It became widely beloved during both World Wars and was used by Mother Teresa, Desmond Tutu, Margaret Thatcher, and many others as an anthem of peace and service.

Lord, make me an instrument of your peace:
Where there is hatred, let me sow love;
Where there is injury, pardon;
Where there is doubt, faith;
Where there is despair, hope;
Where there is darkness, light;
And where there is sadness, joy.

O Divine Master, grant that I may
Not so much seek to be consoled as to console,
To be understood as to understand,
To be loved as to love.

For it is in giving that we receive,
It is in pardoning that we are pardoned,
And it is in dying that we are born to eternal life.
Amen.

The Serenity Prayer

Reinhold Niebuhr, 1932

Written by Protestant theologian Reinhold Niebuhr around 1932, this prayer became famous through Alcoholics Anonymous starting in 1941 and has since become one of the most widely known prayers worldwide.

Short Version:

God, grant me the serenity
To accept the things I cannot change,
The courage to change the things I can,
And the wisdom to know the difference.
Amen.

Full Version:

God, give us grace to accept with serenity
the things that cannot be changed,
Courage to change the things
which should be changed,
and the Wisdom to distinguish
the one from the other.

Living one day at a time,
Enjoying one moment at a time,
Accepting hardship as a pathway to peace,
Taking, as Jesus did,
This sinful world as it is,
Not as I would have it,
Trusting that You will make all things right,
If I surrender to Your will,
So that I may be reasonably happy in this life,
And supremely happy with You forever in the next.
Amen.

Prayer of St. Ignatius of Loyola

St. Ignatius of Loyola, 16th Century (1491-1556)

St. Ignatius, founder of the Jesuits, used this prayer to encourage total commitment to God and service.

Dearest Lord, teach me to be generous;
Teach me to serve You as You deserve;
To give and not to count the cost,
To fight and not to heed the wounds,
To toil and not to seek for rest,
To labor and not to ask for reward
Save that of knowing I am doing Your will.
Amen.

The Suscipe (Take, Lord, Receive)

St. Ignatius of Loyola, 16th Century

Another profound prayer from St. Ignatius, expressing complete surrender to God. This prayer appears in his Spiritual Exercises and is often prayed during retreats and at times of major life decisions.

Take, Lord, and receive all my liberty,
My memory, my understanding,
And my entire will,
All I have and call my own.

You have given all to me.
To You, Lord, I return it.

Everything is Yours; do with it what You will.
Give me only Your love and Your grace.
That is enough for me.
Amen.

Prayer of St. Richard of Chichester

St. Richard of Chichester, 13th Century (1197-1253)

A simple yet profound prayer asking to know, love, and follow Christ more deeply. Made famous in the modern era through the musical "Godspell."

Thanks be to thee, my Lord Jesus Christ,
For all the benefits thou hast given me,
For all the pains and insults thou hast borne for me.

O most merciful redeemer, friend and brother,
May I know thee more clearly,
Love thee more dearly,
And follow thee more nearly,
Day by day.
Amen.

The Jesus Prayer

Early Desert Fathers, 4th-5th centuries

One of the oldest and most central prayers in Eastern Orthodox Christianity, dating back to the early Christian Desert Fathers. Used continuously as a form of contemplative prayer, often repeated hundreds or thousands of times with a prayer rope.

Full Version:

Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God,
Have mercy on me, a sinner.
Amen.

Shorter Variations:

Lord Jesus Christ, have mercy on me.

Lord Jesus, have mercy.

Lord, have mercy. (Kyrie eleison)

This prayer condenses the Christian faith into a single breath—containing confession, petition, and orthodox Christology. It's designed for continuous repetition and has been the foundation of Orthodox contemplative practice for over 1,600 years.

Anima Christi (Soul of Christ)

14th Century

A deeply devotional prayer traditionally associated with St. Ignatius of Loyola, though it predates him. Often prayed after receiving Communion.

Soul of Christ, sanctify me.
Body of Christ, save me.
Blood of Christ, inebriate me.
Water from the side of Christ, wash me.
Passion of Christ, strengthen me.

O good Jesus, hear me.
Within Thy wounds hide me.
Suffer me not to be separated from Thee.
From the malicious enemy defend me.
In the hour of my death call me,
And bid me come unto Thee,
That with Thy saints I may praise Thee
Forever and ever.
Amen.

St. Patrick's Breastplate

Attributed to St. Patrick of Ireland, 5th Century (c. 385-461)

An ancient Celtic prayer of protection, traditionally attributed to St. Patrick as he traveled through Ireland evangelizing. Also known as "The Lorica" (Latin for breastplate or armor).

I arise today
Through a mighty strength, the invocation of the Trinity,
Through belief in the Threeness,
Through confession of the Oneness
Of the Creator of creation.

I arise today
Through the strength of Christ's birth with His baptism,
Through the strength of His crucifixion with His burial,
Through the strength of His resurrection with His ascension,
Through the strength of His descent for the judgment of doom.

Christ with me, Christ before me, Christ behind me,
Christ in me, Christ beneath me, Christ above me,
Christ on my right, Christ on my left,
Christ when I lie down, Christ when I sit down, Christ when I arise,
Christ in the heart of every man who thinks of me,
Christ in the mouth of every man who speaks of me,
Christ in every eye that sees me,
Christ in every ear that hears me.

I arise today
Through a mighty strength, the invocation of the Trinity,
Through belief in the Threeness,
Through confession of the Oneness
Of the Creator of creation.

St. Augustine's Evening Prayer

St. Augustine of Hippo, 4th-5th Century (354-430)

A beautiful prayer asking God's presence during the night hours, especially for those awake, keeping vigil, or in distress.

Watch, O Lord, with those who wake, or watch, or weep tonight,
And give Your angels charge over those who sleep.

Tend Your sick ones, O Lord Christ.
Rest Your weary ones.
Bless Your dying ones.
Soothe Your suffering ones.
Pity Your afflicted ones.
Shield Your joyous ones.

And all for Your love's sake.
Amen.
